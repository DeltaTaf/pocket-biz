Why Pocket Biz?

Entering a new market is difficult because essential information is scattered across legal websites, reports, blogs, country guides, and personal experiences. Pocket Biz brings the practical pieces together, because apparently businesses prefer not to discover cultural mistakes after paying invoices.

Culture + law + market

Country profiles explain etiquette, regulations, opportunities, risks, mistakes, costs, and official resources.

Market-entry contacts

Local providers can apply to be listed for company setup, tax, law, translation, relocation, and market research.

Community + AI review

Users can submit real experiences, then AI can help filter, summarize, categorize, and flag weak or biased submissions.

Top Mistakes Foreigners Make

🇯🇵 Japan

Rushing decisions, being too direct too early, treating business cards casually, assuming silence means agreement.

🇸🇪 Sweden

Overselling aggressively, ignoring planning, being late, or acting superior because of title.

🇬🇷 Greece

Ignoring relationships, relying only on verbal agreements, expecting bureaucracy to move instantly.

Cost of Market Entry

Basic preparation

Market research, translated documents, cultural preparation, and initial legal consultation.

Setup support

Company formation, tax registration, local accountant, workspace, permits, and visa/work support.

Growth support

Local partner search, distributor contacts, business development, advertising, and customer research.

Market Entry Timeline

Week 1

Week 2

Contacts

Find legal, tax, translation, and market-entry providers.

Week 3

Preparation

Prepare documents, meetings, pitch material, and market assumptions.

Week 4

Entry

Founder Story

Built from an International Business problem

Pocket Biz started while studying International Business and noticing how many factors companies must consider before entering a new market: culture, laws, consumer behavior, etiquette, risks, partners, and local support. The idea is to make that preparation clearer, faster, and more practical.
